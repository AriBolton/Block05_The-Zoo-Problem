Wild Animal Mini-Zoo Overview

Bears

Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight, and hearing. They can smell food, cubs, predators, and mates from miles away.

Bears are mammals that belong to the family Ursidae. They can be as small as four feet long and about 60 pounds (the sun bear) to as big as eight feet long and more than a thousand pounds (the polar bear). Giraffes

Giraffes are the tallest land animals on Earth, with males reaching heights of up to 18 feet and females up to 14 feet.

There are four distinct species of giraffe: Northern giraffe Giraffa camelopardalis, Southern giraffe giraffa, Reticulated giraffe G. reticulata and Masai giraffe G. tippelskirchi. Giraffes are already extinct in at least seven countries in Africa. Below is a list of the giraffes who are currently residing in our zoo.

  • Frankie
  • Coconut

Lions

For all of their roaring, growling, and ferociousness, lions are family animals and truly social in their own communities. They usually live in groups of 15 or more animals called prides. Below is a list of the lions who are currently residing in our zoo.

  • Mella
  • Karl

Monkeys

Monkeys are known to use tools, such as leaves and stones, to accomplish tasks. Capuchin monkeys are considered to be the most intelligent New World monkeys and are known for their ability to use and make tools.

There are many different things that come to mind when we think about Monkeys. They are often seen as being cute and highly intelligent animals. There are many different species that have their own characteristics in terms of size, color, location, and even their abilities. They are known for being good care givers to the young and even having some humanistic movements. Below is a list of the monkeys who are currently residing in our zoo.

  • Cookie
  • Earl
  • Banana Pudding

Alligators

Alligators don't have sex-determining chromosomes, so the temperature at which they hatch determines their gender. The first 10 days of development are especially important.

American alligators once faced extinction. The United States Fish and Wildlife Service placed them on the endangered species list in 1967. Fortunately, the legal protection worked. Just 20 years later, American alligators were taken off the list. Below is a list of the alligators who are currently residing in our zoo.

  • Wren
  • Aspen
  • Mika
